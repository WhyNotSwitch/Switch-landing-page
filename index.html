Our Reason

The majority of domestic work is done by women and girls. That domestic work becomes more difficult, time consuming, and less productive in the absence of electricity.

Women would be able to escape the burden of many home chores if they had access to electricity. Even after dark, it would give them extra time for educational or economic activities. Because women are the primary users of household energy equipment, it makes sense to prioritize their needs when developing projects aimed at reducing energy poverty, allowing them to participate in a more prosperous and equitable economy.
